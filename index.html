Sculpt of an OC
Retopology of original model
Comparison of sculpt and retopology

Made in Blender

Over a weekend I decided to teach myself retopology, rebuilding a 3D model with fewer polygons optimized for game design or animation. After practicing with existing models from video games and resource packs, I thought I'd try it with a sculpt of my own!

As you can see, the retopology has less detail than the original sculpt, but the original sculpt has literally millions of polygons. This makes it difficult for the machine to render quickly in a game or animation environment, so it is advisable to build a slightly simpler retopology for the engine to be able to render the model more easily.

Rebecca from Cyberpunk: Edgerunners
Rebecca's hairclips

Made in Daz Studio, Blender, Adobe Photoshop

I've been practicing texturing for character design, developing a kind of painterly style. I prefer more cartoonish styles to realism, taking inspiration from shows like Arcane.

My process for texturing takes place mostly in Blender, which has fairly decent texturing tools. If I need a texture to be more precise, like with the eyes, I'll mark the location on the UV map in Blender and bring it into Photoshop.

The hair, hairclips, and textures are all original.

Console for an AI

Made in Blender

Everyone has an opinion on AI nowadays. Developers are trying very hard to replicate organic neural networks in code. In the story I'm working on, these developers have access to quantum computers and donor brain cells. I needed a "character" design that properly conveyed the horror of a computer that effectively runs on human flesh. I opted for a "Hal 9000 meets XBox" kind of design, a commercial console designed for user appeal but still dangerous looking. I also took inspiration from older car radios, because the aesthetic I'm going for is very neo-eighties.

ESO Alchemy's Mask

Made in Blender, Adobe Photoshop

Alchemy is one of my favorite characters in ESO. She's a High Elf, a clown, and she's transgender. Her story is sad, she ran away from home because she was being forced into an identity and occupation that wasn't right for her. This story resonated with the LGBTQ community, and in 2019, ESO: Summerset won a GLAAD award for Outstanding Video Game.

I like the character and I like her design, so I remade her mask for use in TES V: Skyrim.

Upcycled Mermaid Skirt

A while back, at a sidewalk fair outside my apartment, I got half a yard of this fun fabric from someone selling scrap fabric. I had no idea what to do with it, so it sat in my scrap fabric bag for a few years. Then, in 2023, I randomly got the idea to turn it into a cute skirt. I was concerned I wouldn't have enough fabric, but I still managed to make the skirt suitably swishy. When sewing, I generally try to use as much of the fabric I have as I can, minimizing waste. Sustainability is important to me as a seamstress and designer, and my work reflects that.

Alternate Start - The Last Dwemer

Made in Nifskope, Creation Kit

This is the first big mod I've released on the Nexus. Before then, most of my mods had been small tweaks and patches for myself. But this one was different, I polished it and put it up online and got tons of downloads overnight. Since then, I've gained over 400 endorsements and over 20k unique downloads.

The mod's scenario is simple, the player is a time-traveler that has been underground for a few thousand years. I placed a small room inside a building deep underground in Blackreach with enough starter gear to get the player to the surface. A big draw for the Elder Scrolls is its use of magitech to add sci-fi elements to the medieval fantasy setting, and I'm a big fan of that!

Lady Dimitrescu - Resident Evil: Village

Around 2021-2022, one of the antagonists of Resident Evil: Village soared in popularity among queer fans on then-Twitter. Lady Dimitrescu gained a sort of cult following, a fandom of her own. I was one of those fans, so naturally I decided to cosplay her (despite being only 5'3").

The dress is a t-shirt dress I got second-hand, and altered by hand on the floor of my dorm room at Sarah Lawrence. Most of my cosplays are second-hand garments that have been altered to look like what the character wears. For the sake of sustainability, I get much of my clothes and fabric second-hand to reduce waste, and try to be mindful of where I source my materials from.

Pentagram-shaped elastic harness

In 2021, I gave my old Etsy a revamp, and this was one of the first items I posted. Pentagram harnesses were very popular at that time, so I decided to design one for my shop. It's made of quality lingerie elastic and very stretchy. In order to accommodate more sizes, my products are mostly made-to-order. Inclusive sizing is very important to me as someone who's struggled to find a bra that fits for most of their life, so I'm making sure that nobody has that issue with my products. Everyone deserves to have cute clothes, I believe this strongly.
